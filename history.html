History of the Russian Language



The Russian language is the most widely spoken of all the Slavic languages. A descendant of the Indo-European language family, modern Russian began to take shape in the 15th and 16th centuries. By the 19th century, Russian literature gained popularity with the works of Tolstoy and Doystoyevsky. During the Soviet period of the 20th century, spelling reforms, new political terminology, and social changes shaped the Russian language that is used today.
